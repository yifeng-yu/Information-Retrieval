Heres why nominating more actors wouldnt fix Oscar diversity

By Kyle KimGraphics and Data Journalist 
Jan. 22, 2016
2:46 PM

An Oscars statue on the red carpet. (Associated Press)

The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences announced new membership rules today to increase diversity a second year of nominating only white actors.

There had been speculation that the organization would expand the number of nominees for some categories. But would such a change in rules help the Academy's diversity problem?

Of the 25 nominees in acting and directing categories this year, director Alejandro G. Iñárritu is the only non-white person. By examining the actors and directors that Times critics identified as having received Oscars buzz during the awards season without being nominated, we found the racial diversity of nominees increased only from 4% to 8%.
